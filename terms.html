Kalo Policies and Procedures

SECTION 1 - CORPORATE MISSION STATEMENT

Our mission is to create an environment where our employees, Consultants and company managers are aligned to accomplish one goal, to build beautiful lives.  We will do this by offering quality products at a fair price; by treating everyone we come in contact with respect and will do all we can to treat our customers as family.

Kalo, LLC and its affiliated brands. By accepting this contract with Kalo, LLC, you are authorized to distribute selected products.

 

2.1 - What’s in the Consultant Agreement?

Kalo Policies and Procedures are considered part of the Consultant Agreement.

In its entirety, the Consultant Agreement includes:

  1. The Consultant Application and Agreement;
  2. The Policies and Procedures;
  3. The Kalo Compensation Plan (Pay Plan); and
  4. The Kalo Business Entity Registration Form (if applicable).

 

When Kalo refers to the Consultant Agreement or Agreement, it refers to all components as described above. Likewise, when you sponsor a new Consultant, it is your responsibility to ensure they have read and understand the Agreement they are signing, particularly these Policies and Procedures and the Kalo Compensation Plan, before they sign.

The Policies and Procedures are designed to define the relationship that exists between you (as an Consultant), Kalo and other Kalo Consultants, and to clearly articulate a standard for acceptable business conduct. By signing the Agreement, you are required to comply with all of its Terms and Conditions, with these Policies and Procedures, and with all federal, state, and local laws governing your Kalo business and conduct.

 

If you have any questions regarding any policy or rule, do not hesitate to ask your Sponsor or contact Kalo Consultant Support by emailing customersupport@Kalo.com.

If any part of the Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, only that invalid or unenforceable portion may be removed and the remainder of the Agreement shall remain intact and in force.

SECTION 3 - BECOMING AN CONSULTANT

 3.1 - Requirements to Become an Consultant

To become a Kalo Consultant, you must:

 

  1. Be of the age of majority in your state of residence or country;
  2. Reside in a geographical area where Kalo has been approved for business;
  3. Have a valid Social Security or Federal Tax ID number;
  4. Purchase a Starter Kit (except where prohibited by law);
  5. Read and agree to abide by the Company’s Statement of Policies and Procedures; and
  6. Submit a properly completed Consultant Application and Agreement to Kalo.

 

New Consultants enroll online at the Personal Website of a Sponsor. If you do not have a Sponsor and you would like one, please go to the Consultant locator found on the Kalo.com website.

 

You must complete the Consultant Agreement and agree to all terms and related documents yourself. An existing Consultant and/or your Sponsor must not fill out, sign, or accept the agreement on your behalf. See Section 7.4 for more information on sponsoring. Kalo reserves the right to reject any new Consultant Application and Agreement.

 

3.1.1 - Country of Residence

You must be a legal resident of the United States, or, if you are not a permanent resident, you must be legally authorized to work in the United States in order to enroll as a Kalo Consultant. If it is discovered that an individual has provided Kalo with false residency information or false tax ID information during enrollment, their account will be closed and they will lose any commissions not already paid out. Kalo communication, in any form, cannot encourage individuals to complete the enrollment process who are not legally authorized to work in countries where Kalo is registered.

 

3.2 - Can a Business Enroll as an Consultant?

Certain business entities can apply to be a Kalo Consultant by submitting a Business Entity Registration Form and an Consultant Application and Agreement to Kalo, and by following instructions on the Team Officeâ.  When “you”, “your”, and “Consultant” are used in these Policies and Procedures, they refer both to individuals and business entities.

 

Any person or entity with a legal or equitable interest in the Consultant business agrees to all of the terms of the Agreement and these Policies and Procedures. If such person or entity violates or is out of compliance with the terms of the Agreement then the entire Consultant business is out of compliance and subject to disciplinary action as a single entity.

 

If you joined Kalo as an individual but wish to change your status to a business entity, you may do so by following the steps outlined in Section 5.2.1.

 

3.3 - Identification

Upon request or during the application process, you may be asked to provide your Social Security number (SSN) or a federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) to Kalo. Your legal name and Social Security number must be entered as they appear on your Social Security card. Kalo is required by law to provide this information to the IRS for 1099 reporting purposes. Failure to comply may result in suspension or cancellation of your Kalo Account.

 

Once your application is submitted and accepted, Kalo will assign you a unique identification number, known as an Consultant ID Number that will be used by you and/or Consultant Support to place orders and track commissions and bonuses. Passwords consultant with these unique numbers should not be shared with others.

When your Consultant Application and Agreement has been accepted by Kalo, you have the right to:

 

  1. Sell products;
  2. Participate in the Kalo Compensation Plan;
  3. Recruit and sponsor other Consultants, Preferred & Retail Customers (build your downline);
  4. Receive Kalo literature and other communications;
  5. Access Kalo-sponsored support, training, motivational, and recognition functions;
  6. Participate in Kalo-sponsored promotional and incentive contests and programs; and
  7. Receive access to an online ConsultantTeam Officeâ that facilitates and records your business interactions with Kalo.

 

3.5 - Renewal of Your Kalo Business

When you join Kalo and sign the Consultant Agreement you also paid a registration fee which provided access to your Team Office and other support materials sometimes referred to as a starter kit.  No one is required to purchase products to become a Kalo Consultant however the registration is required.  Each year upon your enrollment anniversary the registration is automatically renewed for another one-year term, unless either you or Kalo provide the other party written notice of their intention not to renew the Agreement.

 

SECTION 4 - CONSULTANT RELATIONSHIP WITH KALO

 

4.1 - Independent Contractor Status

When you join Kalo as an Consultant you are an independent contractor. You are not purchasing a franchise or a business opportunity and the Agreement between you and Kalo does not create an employer/employee relationship, partnership, or joint venture. As a result, you are solely responsible for paying all local, state, and federal taxes owed from any compensation earned. Additionally, Kalo will not treat you as an employee for federal tax purposes. This means that Kalo will not withhold FICA or taxes of any kind from your bonuses and commissions.

 

As an independent contractor, you completely control the means by which you operate your Kalo businesses. You will establish your own goals, hours, and methods of sale — subject to compliance with the Agreement and applicable law. You are solely responsible for paying all expenses incurred in developing your business and are not authorized to incur any debt or obligation on behalf of or in the name of Kalo or other Consultants, or to open any checking account on behalf of, for, or in the name of Kalo.

 

4.2 - Non-Solicitation of Kalo Employees

Kalo works hard to ensure it provides the best possible experience for its Consultants. In order to make this possible, we recruit and train employees for your benefit.  Because it is expensive to recruit and train employees and critical for these employees to remain loyal to Kalo and to you, you are expressly prohibited, without Kalo’s prior written permission, from soliciting, hiring, offering employment or compensation of any kind to, or otherwise using any services of any Kalo employee outside of the scope of his/her employment during the term of said employment.

 

4.3 - No Territory Restrictions or Franchise Fees

There are no exclusive territories granted to anyone for selling, recruiting, or marketing. No franchise fees are required.

 

4.4 - Income Taxes

Kalo will provide an IRS Form 1099 MISC (Non-Employee Compensation) earnings statement to each U.S. Consultant who had earnings of $600 or more in the previous calendar year. You are solely responsible for paying local, state, and federal taxes on any income generated as an Consultant.

 

4.5 - Timely Reporting of Errors

If you believe errors have been made regarding commissions, bonuses, charges, or the placement of Consultants in your downline organization, you must notify Kalo in writing within sixty (60) days of when the purported error or incident occurred. After sixty (60) day, payment, bonuses, rewards or charges are considers valid and may not be disputed, unless the payment, bonus, reward or charge was in excess of what Kalo should have awarded.

 

 

4.6 - Limitation of Liability

By signing the Consultant Agreement, you agree to release, discharge, and hold harmless Kalo, and anyone directly affiliated with Kalo (employees, board of directors, officers, etc.), from any losses or damages, including costs and fees, incurred or suffered by you as a result of:

 

  1. Your breach of the Consultant Agreement (including these Policies and Procedures);
  2. The improper promotion or operation of your Kalo business and any related activities (e.g., the presentation of Kalo’s products or Compensation Plan, the operation of a motor vehicle, the lease of meeting or training facilities, web sites or Internet tools, including social media, etc.);
  3. Any incorrect data or information provided by an Consultant to Kalo; or
  4. Your failure to provide any information or data necessary for Kalo to operate its business.

 

4.7 - Requests for Records

Consultant requests for copies of invoices, applications, downline reports, or other records will require a minimum fee of $2.00 and $1.00 per page per copy.

 

4.8 - Roll-Up of Downline Organization

Kalo reserves the right to purge its database of an inactive or terminated Consultant(s). When this happens, Kalo may (or may not) modify the database in a manner that everyone shifts up one level; so the first level (or frontline) of the cancelled Consultant now becomes the first level (or frontline) of the cancelled Consultant’s Sponsor. Where there is the possibility of there being multiple Sponsors, Kalo reserves the right to choose who the new frontline Sponsor will be.

 

4.9 - Sale, Transfer, or Assignment of a Kalo Business

Kalo allows for the sale or transfer of your business in the event you wish to retire from direct selling as defined below. Although your Kalo business is privately owned and independently operated, the Agreement, including these Policies and Procedures, is an agreement between you and Kalo. You may not assign any of the rights you are granted under the Agreement, without first obtaining written authorization from Kalo. Such authorization is at Kalo’s discretion, and will only be granted if, in Kalo’s opinion, the following criteria are met:

 

  1. Protection of the existing line of Sponsorship must always be maintained so that the Kalo business continues to be operated in that line of Sponsorship.  The buyer must, in the opinion of Kalo, be qualified and have the skills, resources, and experience to appropriately carry on the seller’s business and to manage the seller’s downline.
  2. The buyer or transferee must be (or must become) a qualified Kalo Consultant and be approved by Kalo. If the buyer is a current Kalo Consultant, he or she must first terminate their Kalo business simultaneously with the purchase, transfer, assignment, or acquisition of any interest in the new Kalo business.
  3. Before the sale, transfer, or assignment can be finalized and approved by Kalo, any debt obligations the selling Consultant has with Kalo must be satisfied.
  4. The selling Consultant must be in good standing and not in violation of any of the terms of the Agreement in order to be eligible to sell, transfer, or assign a Kalo business.
  5. The selling Consultant must agree not to solicit or entice any other Consultants or Customers within the organization to move to a new position, if they rejoin Kalo under the same or new name.
  6. The seller agrees not to solicit or entice any other Consultants or Customers into another direct selling company for a period of twelve (12) months following the effective date of the transfer. Any violation will result in the cancellation of the transferred account and disputes arising from the violation are between buyer and seller. Both buyer and seller must agree in writing to this provision.
  7. Both the Seller and the Buyer must execute the Account Transfer Form, agreeing to the terms set forth here (located in the Resources tab, Business Builders section, in your Team Office) and email it to customersupport@Kalo.com

 

Prior to selling your Kalo business, you must notify Kalo’s Compliance Department of your intent to sell. Upon complete execution of the purchase and sale agreement, you must submit a copy of it to Kalo for review. Kalo reserves the right to request additional documentation that may be necessary to analyze the transaction between the buyer and seller. Kalo will, at its sole and absolute discretion, approve or deny the sale, transfer, or assignment. If you fail to obtain Kalo’s approval for the transaction, the transfer shall be voidable at Kalo’s option.

 

The purchaser of the existing Kalo business will assume the obligations and position of the selling Consultant. Additionally, if you sell your Kalo business you will not be eligible to re-apply as a Kalo Consultant for at least six (6) full months after the date of the sale.

 

4.10 - Separation of a Kalo Business due to Divorce or Business Break-Up

When a Kalo business is jointly owned and operated by a husband-wife partnership, or across a business entity, there must be a way to equitably separate that Kalo business in the event of a divorce or the dissolution of the business entity. This separation must be handled in a way that does not adversely affect the interests and income of other businesses up or down the line of sponsorship. If the separating parties fail to provide for the best interests of other Consultants and the Company, Kalo will, at its sole discretion, terminate the Consultant and cancel the Consultant Agreement.

 

During the pendency of a divorce or business break-up, you must adopt one of the following methods of operation:

 

  1. One of the parties may, with consent of the other(s), operate the Kalo business pursuant to an assignment in writing whereby the relinquishing spouse, shareholders, partners, or trustees authorize Kalo to deal directly and solely with the other spouse or non-relinquishing shareholder, partner, or trustee.
  2. The parties may continue to operate the Kalo business jointly on a business-as-usual basis, whereupon all compensation paid by Kalo will be paid in the joint names of the Consultants or in the name of the entity to be divided as the parties may independently agree between themselves.

 

In the event that you are unable to resolve a dispute over the disposition of commissions and ownership of the business, your Consultant Agreement will be involuntarily cancelled. Once a spouse or former business affiliate has completely given up their rights in their original Kalo business, they are free to enroll under any Sponsor of their choosing and develop their new business the same as any other new Consultant.

 

4.11 - Succession Due to Death or Incapacitation

In the event of your death or incapacitation, your Kalo business may be passed to your heirs. For this to occur, the necessary legal documentation must be submitted to customersupport@Kalo.com. If you wish to bequeath your Kalo business, please work with an attorney to prepare a will (or other testamentary instrument). If you transfer your Kalo business in this manner, your beneficiary acquires the right to collect all bonuses and commissions from your downline organization, provided certain qualifications are met. The successor(s) must:

 

  1. Execute a new Consultant Agreement;
  2. Comply with terms and provisions of the Agreement; and
  3. Meet all of the qualifications for the deceased Consultant’s rank.

 

Bonuses and commissions of a Kalo business transferred in this manner will be paid in a single installment. If the business is bequeathed to joint devisees, they must form a business entity and acquire a federal Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN). Kalo will issue all bonus and commission checks and one IRS Form 1099 to the business entity.

 

4.11.1 - Transfer Upon Death of an Consultant

To effect a testamentary transfer of a Kalo business, the successor must provide the following to Kalo:

 

  1. An original death certificate;
  2. A notarized copy of the will or other instrument establishing the successor’s right to the Kalo business; and
  3. A completed and executed Consultant Agreement.

5.3.1.1 - Monthly Volume Requirements

SECTION 15 - DEFINITIONS

 

Active Consultant - All downline Consultants who earn 100 points or more in Personal Volume (PV) in a given month. Kalo Consultants begin every month being inactive. They become active each month when they earn 100 points in PV.

Agreement - The contract between the Company and each Consultant that includes the Consultant Application (including Terms & Conditions), the Agreement (including Policies and Procedures), the Kalo Compensation Plan, and the Business Entity Registration Form (where applicable). These documents are collectively referred to as the Agreement.

Bonus - The compensation paid to an Consultant for leadership activities and earned incentives, as provided in the Kalo Compensation Plan.

Cancellation - The termination of an Consultant’s business. Cancellation may be either voluntary or involuntary as a result of non-renewal or inactivity.

Commission - The compensation paid to an Consultant for the sales of commissionable Kalo products as explained in the Kalo Compensation Plan.

Commissionable Products - All Kalo products on which commissions and bonuses are paid.

Customer - An individual who purchases Kalo products through an Consultant.

Commissionable Volume (CV) - The Kalo International business model is built around the retail potential of our product. In order to maintain competitive pricing at the retail level and to ensure a profitable wholesale-to-retail margin for our Consultants, we assign a point value to all products. The CV is the point value assigned to a Kalo product. The compensation program is based on the accumulation of these points. Kalo gives the maximum points possible to create the ideal balance between significant retail profits and substantial override income for our Consultants. Every time product is sold, CV points are generated.

Downline - Your downline consists of Consultants whom you have personally enrolled and of the Consultants they have enrolled and so on. In the Binary Tree, your downline may also include Consultants from Enrollers above you, who have elected to place an individual within your downline organization.

Downline Organization - See Group.

Downline Report - A real-time report generated by Kalo and accessed through each Consultant’s Team Officeâ that provides critical data relating to the identities of Consultants, sales information, and enrollment activity of each Consultant’s downline organization. This report contains confidential and trade secret information that is proprietary to Kalo.

Enrollment Tree - The Enrollment Tree contains Consultants who are directly related to a specific position and is unlimited in width.  The 1st level is comprised of your personally enrolled Consultants; the 2nd level consists of Consultants that are enrolled by 1st level Consultants; and continues through 9 Levels.

Frontline - All Consultants in the first level of an Consultant’s Enrollment Tree.  See Enrollment Tree.

Generation - The arrangement of the Enrollment Tree as explained in the Compensation Plan.

Group - An Consultant and his or her entire downline.

Household - People residing in the same dwelling, regardless of familial relation.

Immediate Household - Heads of household, their spouses or significant others, and dependent members residing in the same house are all immediate household members.

Leg - Each downlineConsultant and their respective group represents one leg in your downline organization.

Level - The layers of Consultants in a particular Consultant’s downline. This term refers to the relationship of an Consultant relative to a particular upline Consultant in the Binary Tree, determined by the number of individuals between Consultants who are related by sponsorship. For example, if A sponsors B, who sponsors C, who sponsors D, who sponsors E; then E is on A’s fourth level.  See also Enrollment Tree/Compensation Plan for additional explanation.

Nonpublic Information - Any information related to Kalo that has not been announced publicly by Kalo. This includes, but is not limited to, information about new products, processes, equipment, territories or sales areas, business changes, products or product lines, personnel, intellectual property, and promotions.

Personal Group Volume (PGV) - Is the total commissionable volume generated through the Consultant’s business center and the Consultants he or she has personally enrolled. In other words, the sum of the Personal Group Volume (PGV) of all members of a team.

Personal Volume (PV) - The value of all commissionable products sold to an Consultant or to an Consultant’s customer(s) that meets the requirement for an Consultant to achieve a status of “Active” and to be qualified for commissions as outlined in the Compensation Plan.

Public Announcement - Information shall be deemed to be publicly announced only if it has been released to the media though a press release by Kalo, emailed to Consultants directly from Kalo, communicated to Consultants though a Team Officeâ posting by Kalo, or announced by an authorized Kalo officer at an event sponsored and conducted by Kalo, such as Convention, Boot Camp, World Tour, training phone calls, and Leader phone calls. Events sponsored by Consultants, Leaders, etc., are not Kalo events. Appearance of information on forums, even forums hosted on the Kalo corporate website, does not constitute an official announcement.

Personal Website - Refers to the external facing Consultant website offered by Kalo to as part of your registration fee.  Also known as a “replicated website”.

Registered External Website - Refers to your own Kalo approved external website (if you have one) or other Kalo approved Web presence that is hosted on non-Kalo servers and has no affiliation with Kalo.

Rank - The monthly qualification level at which an Consultant’s compensation will be determined. Rank changes (up or down) based on performance.

Reinstatement - The reactivation of an account that has been cancelled for any reason.

Resalable - Products and sales aids shall be deemed resalable if each of the following elements is satisfied:

  1. They are unopened and unused;
  2. Packaging and labeling has not been altered (including stickers/labels) or damaged;
  3. The product and packaging are in a condition such that it is a commercially reasonable practice within the trade to sell the merchandise at full price; and
  4. Products must be included in the current version of a Kalo catalog or are currently being sold through the Kalo Store web site (Product-of-the-Month discounted, personalized items ordered from Kalo Store, and products sold in packs containing multiple pieces with the package opened are not considered resalable).

Restoration - The reactivation of an account that has been cancelled due to inactivity in which the former downline and title of that account at the time of cancellation may be recaptured.

Roll-Up - The method by which a vacancy in a downline organization left by an Consultant whose Consultant Agreement has been cancelled or by a Sponsor becoming ineligible to retain downline may be filled.

Sponsor - An Consultant who enrolls another Consultant into the Company, and is listed as the Sponsor on the Consultant Application and Agreement. The act of enrolling others and training them to become Consultants is called sponsoring.

Registration/Starter Kit - A selection of Kalo training materials, product samples, and business support literature that each new Consultant is required to purchase.

Suspension - Temporary removal of Consultant privileges (ability to sell and/or recruit). Suspension can be voluntary or involuntary.

Team - An Consultant and his or her downline.

Termination - Permanent removal of Consultant privileges.

Title - The highest Rank achieved by an Consultant to date (see Section 13.6). An Consultant’s title only changes by advancement.

Upline - This term refers to the Consultant or Consultants above a particular Consultant in a sponsorship line upward all the way to the Company. Conversely stated, it is the line of Sponsors that links any particular Consultant to the Company.
